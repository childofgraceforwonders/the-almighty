Your honor, the evidence presented in the scriptures, when read plainly, points to Jesus as God's ultimate prophet, messiah, and exalted son, but not as God Himself. The belief that he is God is a later interpretation that contradicts Jesus's own words and the foundational principles of his faith.

Point 1: Jesus Never Explicitly Claims to Be God. Throughout the Gospels, Jesus is called many things: Messiah, Son of God, Son of Man, Rabbi, Lord. Yet, not once does he utter the simple, unambiguous phrase, "I am God." If this were the central truth of his identity, why would he obscure it? Prophets before him spoke for God, but they never claimed to be God. Jesus follows this prophetic tradition. His claims were of a unique relationship with God, not of being God Himself.

Point 2: Jesus Consistently Subordinates Himself to the Father. Jesus's own words create a clear hierarchy, not an equality.

  • In John 14:28, he states plainly, "The Father is greater than I." This is not a statement of equals. It is a clear declaration of a lesser position.
  • In Matthew 24:36, Jesus admits a limit to his own knowledge, saying only the Father knows the hour of his return. God is, by definition, omniscient (all-knowing). By admitting he doesn't know something the Father knows, Jesus separates himself from the nature of God.
  • In John 20:17, after his resurrection, he tells Mary, "I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God." An entity who has a God cannot, by definition, be that same God.

Point 3: The Core of Jesus's Faith Was Strict Monotheism. Jesus was a devout Jew. The foundation of Judaism is the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4): "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one." This is a belief in a single, indivisible God. The idea of a multi-person Godhead is a concept foreign to the Hebrew Bible. The title "Son of God" was used for righteous men, angels, and the kings of Israel; it signified a special relationship, not a shared divine nature. To make Jesus into God is to violate the very first commandment upon which his entire faith was built.

These points, taken directly from the biblical text, build a powerful case. They show a figure who, while being the most exalted of all creation, knew his place as subordinate to the one true God, the Father.
